Race Fuelling Planner

Set up your race profile with speeds and distances, add your food items to a timeline, and see real-time glucose and fructose absorption rates to optimise your fuelling strategy.

Absorption model

Glucose is absorbed via SGLT1 transporters at a maximum rate of ~60 g/hr. Fructose uses the GLUT5 transporter at ~30 g/hr. Combined 2:1 glucose:fructose products can deliver up to ~90 g/hr of total carbohydrate. With training and gut conditioning, rates up to ~120 g/hr are achievable. Above 120 g/hr you risk GI distress.

The graph uses a rolling 60-minute window — at any point, the rate shown is total grams consumed in the previous 60 minutes. Colour-coded zones: green ≤ 90, amber 90–120 (achievable with gut training), red > 120 g/hr (GI distress risk).

Individual absorption rates vary with training, gut conditioning, and race intensity. Always practise your fuelling strategy in training before race day.
