### Bike — what actually happened

Two-lap structure clearly visible in the data:

- **Lap 1 (first 63 km, 5817 sec ≈ 97 min):** AP 202 W, NP 212, HR avg 142, decoupling 2.3%. **Hit Lap 1 at IF ~0.67 — close to plan target.** Climbed to Bertinoro on the 98s/180s/195s WORK intervals, hit 285–305 W on the climbs.
- **Mid-section (~67 km easy, 6512 sec ≈ 109 min):** AP 167 W, NP 186, HR 141, decoupling -1.8% (negative — recovering). This is the descent and flat section back toward Cervia.
- **Climb 2 attempt (203 sec, 240 W):** Bertinoro lap 2, **already softer than lap 1 climbs** (240 W vs 285–305 W). Aerobic capacity was eroding.
- **Final return (4645 sec ≈ 77 min):** AP 157 W, NP 177, HR 137. **AP dropped from 202 to 157 lap-1-to-lap-2.** That's a 22% AP fade. Significant.

### Bike — diagnosis

Two failure modes, both fixable:

1. **Lap 1 was paced near plan; Lap 2 collapsed.** This is a classic "rode the legs we trained" → "couldn't sustain" pattern. The 14.5% decoupling confirms: HR drifted relative to power, meaning aerobic durability ran out by ~2.5 hr in. **Fix: more long Z2 volume in build, brick-rides at race wattage to harden 4–5 hr aerobic durability.**
2. **Climbs ate matches.** 35 kJ above FTP across two Bertinoro climbs. Fine on lap 1; cost lap 2. **Fix: cap climbs at IF 0.78 (project rule already), even if legs feel willing.**

### Bike — what to track in 2026

- Long-ride aerobic decoupling on every Z2 ride >3 hr. Target trend: 14% → <5% by August.
- 4–5 hr ride at NP 210–225 W. Verify target pace can be held without HR drift.
- VI on every long ride. Target <1.05 in race rehearsal.
- Power-HR Z2 ratio. Last race: 1.33 in Z2. Track trend.

- First 3 splits 4:31 / 4:45 / 4:40 — slight over-pace at the start.

**Km 30–40: walk breaks every 2–3 km, getting longer (avg 5:50/km with intermittent 7–8 min/km blocks, HR 130–144)**
- Intervals 39, 45, 55 (~km 30, 36, 41 — wait, recalc): intervals 39/45/55 around km 19–27 in actual distance. Walk breaks at 6:41, 6:38.
- Intervals 60, 63, 67, 68: walks of 7:08, 8:02, 6:51, 7:31 — these are substantial mid-segment walks, not just aid stations.

**Km 40+: cognitive engagement returns, kick at finish**
- Last several intervals (78–82): 5:34, 5:33, 5:29, 5:10, 5:29, 5:39 — **pace recovers** as the finish line approaches.
- Final 270 m: 4:07/km, HR 163, cadence 89. **The engine was still there.**

### Run — diagnosis

**This was not a glycogen bonk.** A bonk pattern shows: HR rising for declining effort, then crashing to walking-only with no recovery. What this run shows:

- HR consistently *suppressed at constant effort* in the second half (lower HR for same RPE = classic mild dehydration + heat-driven CO suppression + early-phase glycogen sparing).
- Walk-breaks at aid stations were clearly happening from km 13 onwards.
- **The walk breaks were too long.** Many added 60–150 seconds beyond a disciplined "douse, sip, swallow, go" stop.
- Cognitive function was preserved (4:07/km final 270 m, HR 163 — full engagement available).

**Estimated cost of walk-break overrun:** summing the seconds beyond a baseline 150 sec/500 m walking-pace split for each walk-affected interval gives **~15 minutes of unplanned walk-break time** across the marathon. With disciplined aid-station behaviour (~5 min total walk allowance), the run would have been ~3:42 at the same effort. With heat acclimation reducing the suppressed-HR effect by another 5–8 min, **3:30 is on the board.**

### Run — what to track in 2026

- **Aid-station discipline drill** in every long run from June: stopwatch every aid stop, target ≤30 sec.
- Long-run intra-run pace coefficient of variation. Target: tighter than 2025 race.
- HR vs pace decoupling on hot long runs (>22°C) — track it as the heat-tolerance KPI.
- 4 × 10 km loop simulation in build: replicate the lap structure, practise the "don't shorten the engine because of one walk" mindset.

| Bike aerobic durability gain | -2 to -3 min | Decoupling 14.5% → <5% via long Z2 volume. Lifts the IF you can hold across 4:44 of riding. |
| T1+T2 discipline | -2 min | Last year ~5 min each. Drillable. |
| Run aid-station discipline | -8 to -10 min | Direct measurement: ~15 min of walk-break overrun beyond aid-station-disciplined ≤30 sec stops. Targeting ~5 min total walking allowance. |
| Run heat tolerance | -5 to -8 min | The HR suppression in the second half cost pace. Heat acclimation (Zurawlew protocol, mixed-method) measurably restores it. |
| Run fuelling discipline | -2 to -3 min | Liquid-primary, clock-driven, no gels — already the protocol. Cola from km 10 + soft flask + chews. Reinforces aid-station discipline (pre-planned intake means shorter stops). |
| Swim technique | -2 min | Bilateral breathing, sighting, OWS-specific. Already in plan. |

**Sum: −29 to −38 minutes from discipline alone.** This is the **floor** for 9:30 — what's available *with the same fitness as 2025*. Priority hierarchy: heat acclimation > cooling protocol > run aid-station discipline > bike pacing/aero > swim work.

### Adding the fitness ceiling

Athlete is also planning to be fitter in 2026 — higher FTP, better run economy, improved swim form. Those gains stack on top:

| Fitness lever | Expected delta | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| FTP retest end-May / mid-July | +5–10 W on FTP from base building. If FTP rises 316 → 326, NP target at IF 0.71 lifts to 232 W. Bike time: -2 to -3 min on top of pacing fix. |
| Run economy (form work, drills, strength) | -2 to -4 min | Lower O₂ cost at goal pace = HR-headroom in heat = less suppression in second half. Compounds with heat acclimation. |
| Swim form (catch, sighting, bilateral) | -1 to -2 min | Beyond the basic 2 min technical gain already counted. |

**Combined ceiling: discipline -29 to -38 + fitness -5 to -9 = −34 to −47 min.** Realistic banded outcome:

- **Floor (discipline holds, fitness flat):** 9:30
- **Median (discipline + planned fitness):** 9:15–9:25
- **Ceiling (everything works, no heat surprise):** 9:00–9:15
- **Conservative (one thing breaks):** 9:45 = B-goal

The implication for plan design: **9:30 is no longer the goal — it's the lower bound of what's achievable.** Plan-level decisions should optimise for the *median* path (sub-9:25), not just defend the 9:30 floor. That means: protect the FTP retest dates, treat run economy as a measurable deliverable, and don't trade fitness work for "safety" volume in the build.

Suppressed HR at constant effort → cardiac output is being limited by heat + dehydration + early glycogen sparing — appetite suppression is correlated but isn't the primary brake.

## Notes for future Claude

- **The 9:30 goal is a discipline goal, not a fitness goal.** Last year had the engine; the engine never came out. Plan-design decisions should be evaluated against "does this protect race-day execution discipline?" first, "does this build fitness?" second.
- **Aerobic durability (long Z2 volume + decoupling tracking) is the second-order fitness lever.** It enables Lap-2 bike NP to hold without fade, which is the largest single time saving.
- **Aid-station drill is a coachable, measurable behaviour.** Time it in every long run from June. Race-rehearsal stops in the long brick must include the discipline.
