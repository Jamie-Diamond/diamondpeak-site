# Run-leg execution — IM Cervia

**TL;DR:** Patient start (≥10 sec/km slower than goal pace for first 5 km, RPE/HR-led in heat), negative-split intent across the four 10 km loops, clock-driven liquid-primary fuelling (no gels), water dousing at every aid station from km 5, cola from km 10, **aid stops ≤30 sec** (the 2025 failure mode), and HR-cap decision rule for heat. The –20 min target is unlocked by aid-station discipline + heat tolerance, not run fitness.

> **What the 2025 race actually showed** (see `race-day-2025.md`): not a "blow-up at km 25". Engine fully on through km 13. First aid-station walk break at km 13. Walk-breaks every ~5 km from km 13–22, then every 2–3 km, lengthening to 7–10 min/km blocks of nearly 5 minutes each. **~15 min of cumulative walk-break overrun.** HR was *suppressed* (138–144) at constant effort in the second half — heat + early glycogen sparing — not bonk. Final 270 m at 4:07/km, HR 163: **the engine was still there at the finish.** This file is built around the *actual* failure mode, not the "blow-up" narrative.

> Source caveat: parts of this file inherit pacing/fuelling cadence from a marathon template. Numbers do **not** transfer verbatim to an IM run leg off ~5 hrs of cycling. Use the IM-specific rules below; treat the marathon table as principle illustration only.

## Race-morning timeline (project plan rules apply; this is the running-coach rhythm)

| Before start | Action |
|---|---|
| 3 hr | Wake. Eat ~2–3 g CHO/kg (~160–240 g for 79 kg) — proven tolerable food (oats / rice / banana / toast). Coffee if routine (count toward race-day caffeine ceiling). |
| 2.5 hr | Finish eating. Fluids spread across the next 90 min — not all at once. |
| 60–90 min | Arrive transition. Bag drop, kit checks, toilet — go early, queues build. |
| 30 min | 30–40 g CHO (Maurten drink or familiar substitute). Ice slurry 300–500 ml. |
| pre-swim | ~80 mg caffeine (anchored to ~79 kg race weight, ~3 mg/kg total ceiling). |

## IM-run pacing — patient start, aid-station discipline, HR-floor decision rule

| Phase | km | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Patient open | 0–5 km | ≥10 sec/km slower than goal pace. RPE/HR-led, **not** pace-led. Skip the temptation to "make up T2 time". HR ceiling 150. |
| Lock in | 5–20 km (loops 1–2) | Settle to goal pace 4:58/km. Cola from aid station 1 after km 10. **Aid stops ≤30 sec from km 5.** |
| Hold the line | 20–32 km (loops 2–3) | Maintain goal pace. The 2025 collapse started at km 27 — protect this segment with shorter aid stops, not slower pace. |
| Negative split | 32–42.2 km (loop 4) | Whatever's left, but never accelerate before km 32 in heat. |

> **Chase watts, not pace.** Wind, camber, gradient, and surface change pace but not power output. With Garmin native run power (Fenix 8, when wired), discipline by power. Without, discipline by HR + RPE — pace is a lagging indicator in heat.

### HR-floor heat-tolerance decision rule

The 2025 second-half failure was **suppressed HR at constant race-pace effort** — HR drifted *down* from 148 to 138 across the run despite same RPE. That's the heat-tolerance KPI signature: cardiac output is being limited by heat + dehydration + early glycogen sparing.

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| HR rising for goal pace, you're working hard | Normal. Hold pace, hold fuelling cadence. |
| HR *suppressed below 145* at goal-pace effort, RPE rising | Heat-tolerance failure window. **Slow target pace by 5–10 sec/km until HR responds back to 145+ at the new effort.** Increase aid-station dousing (drinking AND dousing every station, not alternating). |
| HR <130 + cognitive dullness + shivering | Heat illness window. Walk to next aid, get full water dousing, sit if available, reassess. Race is over for time goals; finish becomes the goal. |

## IM-run fuelling + aid-station discipline — the 2025 fix

> **NEVER prescribe gels for this athlete.** See `02-conflicts.md` §1.
>
> **2025 cost-of-failure benchmark:** ~15 min of walk-break overrun beyond a disciplined ≤30 sec/stop allowance. **This is the largest single addressable time loss in the workspace.** Train it like a session, not a logistics afterthought.

### Aid-station choreography (drill in every long run from June)

Target time: **≤30 sec at every stop**. Sequence:

1. Slow to walk 50 m before the station, not at it (smoother HR transition).
2. **Grab in order:** water bottle → cola/sports → sponge.
3. **Use in order:** sip cola → swallow → pour water on head → tuck sponge.
4. **Move out** as soon as feet stop dragging — don't wait for "ready".
5. Resume goal pace within 30 m of the station exit.

Discipline rule: **a 30-sec walk-break is a fuel-and-cool stop. Any walk break >30 sec is a tactical failure** (or a heat-illness response — see HR-floor rule above).

### Fuelling timeline

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| First 5 min off bike | First fuel. Before anorexia onset. Pre-loaded soft flask. |
| Every 20 min | Soft-flask sip (Maurten 320 in 250 ml or PF 90). Watch alarm. **Eat by clock, not appetite.** |
| Every aid station from km 5 | Water dousing — head, neck, front of tri-suit, quads. Sponges if available, stuffed inside straps over carotid/brachial pathways. ≤30 sec. |
| Every aid station from km 10 | Cola or course sports drink. Sipped continuously between stations. |
| Backup | Beta Fuel / PF chews in pocket — supplement only, not primary. |

Target rate: **60–75 g CHO/hr** on the run (vs 90–100 g/hr on the bike).

Soft flask refill strategy: stash near transition exit (athlete declines special-needs bags). Cola from aid stations covers the rest.

### Caffeine timeline (race-day, ~3 mg/kg ceiling for 79 kg = ~240 mg total, low habituation)

| Timing | Dose |
|---|---|
| Pre-swim | ~80 mg |
| Final 30 min of bike | ~80 mg |
| Early run (within 5 min of T2) | ~80 mg |
| Halfway run (~21 km) | optional, by feel — total cap reached |

## Heat-driven pacing override

If course temperature ≥27 °C or humidity high:

- First 5 km: HR cap ≥10 bpm below standalone-marathon HR ceiling.
- Allow goal pace to be missed by 5–8 sec/km on patient open. Don't fight the heat early.
- Increase aid-station frequency: dousing AND drinking at every station, not alternating.
- Watch for early sweat-stop: if not sweating despite high effort, walk the next aid station, douse aggressively, take 200 ml fluid + cola, reassess.

## Gear check (universal, do day before)

- Race shoes (already with 30+ km on them; never new on race day).
- Watch charged 100%.
- Fuel counted and distributed: bottles, soft flask, pocket chews.
- Bodyglide — inner thighs, nipples, underarms.
- Race plan written on wrist or arm.
- Tri-suit chosen for race conditions (light colour, water-retentive).
- Sunglasses, race belt, soft flask filled, cap or visor.

## Post-race

Walk 15 min. Get warm — body temp drops fast after IM. Eat within 45 min. No running 10–14 days minimum after an IM marathon.

## The seven blow-up modes (ranked by ultra-DNF data; share % is from source)

Every mode is a marathon and IM-run risk. Rank stands; absolute % is the source's ultra figure.

| Mode | Share | Prevention for this athlete |
|---|---|---|
| GI failure | 23% | Gut-train in long sessions. Rotate carb sources. Stick to own electrolyte — never new aid-station brand mid-race. Maurten + cola + chews already proven. |
| Acute injury | 22% | Single-leg strength work (see `run-form-and-strength.md`). Bone pain vs muscle pain — bone pain stops you immediately. Ankle still in rehab — load discipline applies right through to race day. |
| Pacing error | 18% | First 5 km embarrassingly easy. Chase watts/HR/RPE, not pace. |
| Heat collapse | — | **Project-specific top-3 risk for Cervia.** Heat acclimation 14–20 sessions, water dousing protocol, ice slurry pre-swim and at T2, conservative IF on Bertinoro. |
| Mental | 12% | Never quit at a low point. Eat, walk 2 min if needed, reassess. The low point usually passes within 10 min. |
| Bonk | 10% | Eat *before* hungry. 60–75 g CHO/hr run, 90–100 g/hr bike. Watch alarm every 20 min on the run. |
| Gear failure | 8% | Nothing new on race day. Bodyglide early. Spare gels — n/a (athlete doesn't use). Spare CO2, tubeless plug for the bike leg. |

(Cutoffs deliberately omitted — IM Cervia has no checkpoint cutoffs in play.)
