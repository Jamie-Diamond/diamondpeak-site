# Conflicts — source extract vs live race plan

The reference material in this folder was extracted from a Notion template aimed at standalone marathon and ultra runners. Several assumptions in that source contradict this athlete's live IM plan. **Live project plan wins.** Use this file as the reconciliation table.

## 1. Gels as default fuel — OVERRIDDEN

| Source says | Project plan says |
|---|---|
| "First gel at 20–25 min, then every 20–25 min thereafter. Without fail. Even if not hungry." | Athlete has a sensory aversion to gels (not GI). Run-leg fuel is liquid-primary: 250–500 ml soft flask of concentrated CHO drink (Maurten 320 in 250 ml or PF 90), refilled near transition exit; cola or course sports drink at every aid station from km 10; chews as backup only. |

**Carry over from source:** the *clock-driven, eat-by-time-not-appetite* principle. Watch alarm every 20 min. First fuel within 5 min of leaving T2, before anorexia onset. Target 60–75 g CHO/hr on the run.

## 2. Marathon as a standalone race — OVERRIDDEN for numbers, kept for principles

| Source says | Project plan says |
|---|---|
| Marathon pacing table: 88–92% CP first 10 km → 96–100% CP at 25–35 km. | The IM run starts on already-fatigued legs after ~5 hrs of cycling. Power and pace targets are **not transferable** verbatim. Use the project's IM-run pacing rules instead: first 5 km ≥10 sec/km slower than goal pace, RPE/HR-led in heat for the opener, negative-split intent across the 4 × 10 km loops. |

**Carry over from source:** patient start, chase watts not pace, last-third hold-the-line discipline, "the wall is always avoidable" framing.

## 3. No heat protocol in source — IRRELEVANT (project covers this)

The source has nothing meaningful on heat acclimation or cooling. Heat is the binding constraint on Cervia. The project custom instructions cover the full protocol (post-exercise hot-water immersion à la Zurawlew, indoor trainer in heat, overdressed easy runs in last 3–4 weeks, 14–20 acclimation sessions June–early September, race-week maintenance). **Do not look in this folder for heat advice — go to project instructions.**

## 4. Stryd assumed — OPTIONAL here

| Source says | Project plan says |
|---|---|
| Stryd metrics referenced throughout (form power %, GCT, VO, CP zones). | Stryd is currently optional in the kit list. The Stryd reference (now consolidated into `methodology.md`) is preserved as a *what it would tell you* lookup, not as a current-system requirement. |

If Stryd is added later, the cross-reference table in `run-form-and-strength.md` and the CP-zones table in `methodology.md` are the activation points.

## 5. Subjective wellness fields — IGNORED by athlete decision

Some prompts in `prompts.md` (and the source originally) ask for "subjective freshness 1–10". The athlete does not log these and they are excluded from decision logic. Use objective signals only: HRV (lnRMSSD trend vs 60d baseline), RHR, sleep duration, body weight vs 7-day avg, niggle pain score. The `templates/current-state.md` file captures the few subjective bits that ARE in scope (ankle pain, missed sessions, open actions).

## 6. Special-needs bags assumed — DECLINED

Source race-execution sections sometimes assume special-needs bag access mid-race for a refill. **Athlete declines special-needs bags due to time cost.** All in-race nutrition and cooling must be planned around what is carried, transitioned (T1/T2 own-supplied ice in insulated soft cooler), or available on course.

## 7. Aid-station ice — UNAVAILABLE

Source assumes ice at aid stations. **Cervia confirmed effectively zero ice on course.** Cooling protocol is water-only on course; ice is athlete-supplied via T1/T2 insulated coolers and consumed pre-race / pre-run.

## 8. Half-marathon and ultra material — OUT OF SCOPE

The source covered half-marathon and ultra plans extensively. Dropped during extraction. Don't try to backfill ultra-specific advice into IM execution.

## 9. CSV / calendar UI artefacts — OUT OF SCOPE

The source had Notion-template artefacts (calendar tables, block-map tables, training-history tables) that exist only as UI in the source Notion. Equivalent live data is in Intervals.icu via IcuSync, not here.
