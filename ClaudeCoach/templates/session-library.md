## Bike

### B1. Z2 long ride

- **Stimulus:** aerobic base, mitochondrial density, fuel rehearsal, aero-position practice. **Primary lever for fixing the 2025 Lap 2 fade.**
- **Phase:** all phases. Build duration 3 → 6 hr.
- **Structure:**
  - 15 min easy spin (Z1).
  - Main: ride at 65–75% FTP on flat/rolling terrain. Stay aero on the TT.
  - Optional: insert 3 × 15 min @ 80% FTP in the middle for sub-threshold endurance.
  - Cool 10 min easy.
- **Fuelling rehearsal:** 90–100 g CHO/hr (gut-train toward 120 g/hr in build), ≥800 ml fluid/hr in heat, sodium per protocol.
- **Heat add-on:** post-ride 40 °C bath, 30–40 min, neck-deep (Zurawlew). From late May.
- **Track every session >3 hr:** aerobic decoupling (Pw:HR), VI, AP fade between halves. **Trend target: decoupling 14% (race-day 2025) → <5% by August.**
- **Rationale:** aerobic durability is what enables Lap-2 NP to hold. Race-day 2025 decoupling 14.5% is the diagnosis — long Z2 volume is the prescription.

### B2. Threshold intervals (build phase staple)

- **Stimulus:** raise FTP, lift NP ceiling for race day.
- **Phase:** build (June).
- **Variants** (rotate week to week):
  - 4 × 10 min @ 100% FTP (316 W), 3 min recovery
  - 3 × 15 min @ 95% FTP (300 W), 4 min recovery
  - 2 × 20 min @ 90% FTP (284 W), 5 min recovery
- **Structure:** 15 min warm-up with 3 × 1 min builds → main set → 10 min cool.
- **Indoor preferred** for accuracy. Add fan unless heat layer is the goal.
- **Rationale:** displace aerobic ceiling; +20 W on race-day NP target lives partly here.

### B3. Over-FTP (VO2max)

- **Stimulus:** raise the ceiling that threshold pulls from.
- **Phase:** build, sparingly.
- **Structure:**
  - 5 × 4 min @ 110–120% FTP (348–379 W), 3 min easy recovery.
  - Or 8 × 2 min @ 120–130% FTP (379–411 W), 2 min easy.
- **Frequency:** ≤1×/week. Don't stack with threshold same week if CTL ramp is already at cap.

### B4. Race-pace intervals (specific phase staple)

- **Stimulus:** specific bike fitness for IM NP target ~225 W.
- **Phase:** specific (July).
- **Structure:**
  - 2 × 60 min @ 70–75% FTP (220–237 W) on aero bars, rolling terrain, 10 min recovery between.
  - Or 3 × 40 min @ 70–75% FTP, 8 min recovery.
- **Targets:** IF 0.70–0.75, VI <1.05, decoupling <5%.
- **Rationale:** rehearses race wattage at race position. If decoupling ≥10% → aerobic durability gap → extend Z2 long rides.

### B5. Long brick (race-pace bike + run)

- **Stimulus:** durability under fatigue; T2 transition rehearsal; off-bike running rhythm.
- **Phase:** every 2–3 weeks from June, weekly in July–August.
- **Structure:**
  - 90–120 min bike at IM pace (200–215 W NP).
  - T2 simulation: ≤4 min in race kit.
  - 30–60 min run at IM pace (5:00–5:10/km, RPE 6/10).
  - First km of run ≥15 sec/km slower than target — patient open is the rehearsal.
- **Fuel rehearsal:** soft-flask CHO drink + cola scenario tested here.
- **Rationale:** the IM marathon is *off* the bike — practise that exact starting state.

## Run

> **Ankle gate:** all run sessions pain-led. ≤2/10 during, 0/10 next morning. Quality only after 4 consecutive pain-free weeks. Cap weekly km increase at 10%.

### R1. Easy aerobic run

- **Stimulus:** running economy, aerobic base.
- **Phase:** all phases.
- **Structure:** 30–75 min at conversational pace (Z1, RPE 4/10). Strides optional at end (4 × 20 sec).
- **Cadence:** target 174–178 spm. Use Garmin alert during return-to-run if cadence is dropping.

### R2. Walk-run progression (return-to-run)

- **Stimulus:** load-managed re-introduction post-injury.
- **Phase:** current (ankle in rehab).
- **Structure:**
  - Week 1–2: 30 min total = 5 × (4 min walk / 2 min jog).
  - Week 3–4: 30 min total = 4 × (3 min walk / 4 min jog).
  - Week 5+: 30–40 min continuous easy if pain-free 4 consecutive weeks.
- **Frequency over volume:** 4 short > 2 long for tendon/ligament adaptation.
- **Stop trigger:** any pain >2/10 during.

### R3. Race-pace run intervals

- **Stimulus:** IM-run pacing economy at goal pace.
- **Phase:** specific (July onward, ankle cleared).
- **Structure:** 6 × 1 km @ 4:58/km (3:30 marathon pace), 90 sec jog recovery. Build to 8 × 1 km.
- **Rationale:** neuromuscular pattern for goal pace + economy.

### R4. Tempo run (Daniels T-pace)

- **Stimulus:** lactate threshold, sub-LT durability.
- **Phase:** build, ankle cleared.
- **Structure:**
  - 15 min warm-up.
  - 20–40 min @ T-pace (~half-marathon to 15 km pace, RPE 7/10).
  - 10 min cool.
- **Variant — cruise intervals (Daniels):** 4–6 × 1.6 km @ T-pace, 60 sec rest.

### R5. Long run

- **Stimulus:** marathon-specific endurance, fuel tolerance under fatigue, **aid-station discipline drill**.
- **Phase:** weekly once ankle cleared. 16 → 32 km build.
- **Structure:**
  - First two-thirds easy (Z1–Z2).
  - Final third: insert 3–6 × 1 km at IM pace, or steady at IM pace.
- **Heat layer:** overdressed easy run (final 3–4 weeks of build) for heat exposure.
- **Fuelling rehearsal:** mandatory — practise the soft-flask + cola scenario.
- **Aid-station discipline drill (from June):** every 5 km, simulate an aid station — 30-second stop maximum. Sequence: walk-in 50 m → grab water → cola sip → swallow → pour → tuck sponge → move out within 30 m of station. Stopwatch every stop. **Race-day 2025 leaked ~15 min to walk-break overrun — this drill is the antidote.**
- **Track every session >75 min:** intra-run pace coefficient of variation, HR drift relative to pace, aid-station stop times.

### R6. 4×4 (VO2max)

- **Stimulus:** VO2max top-up, drop-in alongside any methodology.
- **Phase:** sparingly in build/specific, after ankle cleared.
- **Structure:** 4 × 4 min @ 90–95% HRmax (or ~5 km pace if known), 3 min jog recovery.

## Swim

### S1. CSS test

- **Stimulus:** baseline pace anchor; updates all CSS-paced sets.
- **Frequency:** every 8–12 weeks. **Next test: within 2 weeks.** Mid-July retest scheduled.
- **Structure:**
  - 400 m time trial, 5 min rest.
  - 200 m time trial.
  - CSS = (T_400 − T_200) / 2 (per 100 m).
- **Output:** updates Z2/Z3/Z4 pace bands.

### S2. CSS-paced threshold

- **Stimulus:** lactate threshold for swim.
- **Phase:** weekly from now.
- **Variants:**
  - 5 × 200 m @ CSS, 20 sec rest.
  - 10 × 100 m @ CSS, 15 sec rest.
  - 4 × 400 m @ CSS + 5 sec, 30 sec rest.

### S3. Aerobic + technique

- **Stimulus:** stroke quality + base aerobic.
- **Structure:**
  - 400 m easy free.
  - 8 × 50 m drills (catch-up / fingertip drag / side kick / fist).
  - 1500–2000 m steady aerobic.
  - 200 m cool.

### S4. Race-pace open-water swim

- **Stimulus:** OWS-specific skills (sighting, bilateral, draft).
- **Phase:** weekly from June (or earlier if water safe).
- **Structure:**
  - 200 m easy.
  - 3 × 600 m at race effort with sighting every 6–8 strokes; bilateral breathing every set.
  - 200 m cool.
- **Wetsuit:** ≥6 wetsuit sessions before race. Also rehearse non-wetsuit (Adriatic likely warm).

## Heat

### H1. Post-exercise hot-water immersion (primary)

- **Stimulus:** plasma volume expansion, lower core temp at fixed work rate, earlier sweat onset, lower sweat sodium.
- **Phase:** 4–6 sessions/week through July–August.
- **Structure:** 40 °C bath, neck-deep, 30–40 min, immediately post long Z2 ride. Sip plain water during.
- **Validation:** Zurawlew protocol.

### H2. Indoor trainer in heat

- **Stimulus:** active heat training.
- **Phase:** 2×/week in July–August.
- **Structure:** 60–90 min Z2 (205–237 W), warm room, layered clothing, no/minimal fan.

### H3. Overdressed easy run

- **Stimulus:** heat exposure during run, only if ankle robust.
- **Phase:** final 3–4 weeks of build.
- **Structure:** Z1 easy run, long sleeves + base layer, mid-day if possible.

## Brick

See B5 above for the long brick. Short-brick template:

### Br1. Short brick (specific-block dress rehearsal)

- **Stimulus:** off-bike running rhythm.
- **Frequency:** every threshold-bike day in specific phase.
- **Structure:**
  - Bike: planned threshold session (e.g. B2).
  - T2: 3 min — change shoes, soft flask, cap.
  - Run: 15 min @ IM pace (5:00–5:10/km).
- **Goal:** the run starts before the legs feel ready — that's the point.
